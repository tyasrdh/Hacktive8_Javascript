Who created the Coca-Cola logo?

Frank Mason Robinson

The author of the original script logo and the name of the drink was Frank Mason Robinson. While Mr. Robinson worked for the Coca-Cola inventor, John Pemberton, as a bookkeeper, he also played a great part in creating and developing the brand.

The Coca-Cola logo was based on Spencerian font, which was the most widely used form of formal handwriting back then.
